About

I am a Python, and web developer, with professional experience of PHP, Java, and Ruby, and have a keen interest in design, programming languages, and new technology. Since 2007, I have organised Refresh Edinburgh, a monthly meet-up of web workers in Edinburgh, with talks quarterly.

Experience

April 2010 - Present: Co–founder and CTO at Loud Street

I am currently the Co–founder and CTO at Loud Street, where I am responsible for the technical side of the business; from deciding which features we should add, to scheduling, developing, and eventually deploying changes onto our servers.

Eventasaurus is our primary product; a tool to help people organise, and understand their events. Technically, it is a mix of Python, and CoffeeScript, and uses the Django, Celery, jQuery, and Backbone.js frameworks.

April 2009 - Present: Freelance work

I’ve been involved in a handful of freelance projects, most notably, I’ve worked on the ALISS Project for the NHS (Python, Django, MongoDB & Solr) over the last couple of years. I’ve also worked with other freelancers on websites for 7N Architects (Python, Django, HTML, CSS, JavaScript & jQuery), CaSA Architects (Python & Django), and the soon to be completed Quercus Garden Plants (Python & Django), all three of which run a modified version of my own open source CMS.

I also worked on the interaction for the portfolio of my friend Andy Massey (JavaScript).

April 2008 - April 2010: Web Developer at Line Digital

While at Line, I worked on a range of different projects, from the DADI Award winning Digital Podge 2009 website (PHP, Python & JavaScript), to large parts of the AEGON UK (PHP, HTML, CSS & JavaScript), including all of the AEGON Tennis mini-site.

I was also involved with the moderately high-traffic Edinburgh International Film Festival (PHP), large brochure site, Ski Independence (PHP), and the now defunct Tickall (Python & Django), a system for selling RFID tickets for events.

At Line I was responsible for most of the non-trivial front-end work, and occasionally the information architecture of websites.

June 2005 - October 2007: Web Developer at Mercurytide

Originally employed as a Java developer, I was involved in building financial applications for IFAs; clients included Scottish Widows, Prudential, and Standard Life. Later on I was given the opportunity to change the way the company worked, and was instrumental in moving the company from an ageing, proprietary PHP framework to Python, and Django.

After the switch, I worked on several Django sites, including a rebuild of Lettingweb.

July 2003 - June 2004: Intern at Sun Microsystems

Between my third, and fourth years at university, Sun selected me for an internship at their Colorado campus, where I spent most of my time working on Java web-applications for internal customers, and completed the Sun Certification for Java Programmers (SCJP 1.4).
